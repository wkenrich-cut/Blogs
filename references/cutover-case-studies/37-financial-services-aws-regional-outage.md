## Overview

A major global bank used Cutover Respond to orchestrate a 1,800+ person global response to a 35.5-hour AWS US-EAST-1 regional outage caused by a cascading DNS failure, successfully executing 200+ coordinated recovery tasks.

---

## The Problem: An Industry-Stopping Cloud Failure

A Major Global Bank faced an unprecedented crisis when a massive, **35.5-hour regional failure** struck Amazon Web Services (AWS) US-EAST-1. Caused by a cascading DNS failure, the event took key AWS services offline.

The impact spanned Digital Banking and eCommerce to Commercial Lending and Credit Services globally, with hundreds of services potentially impacted. The challenges included:

- **Scale:** Over 1,800 individuals across technology and business teams required coordination
- **Duration:** 35.5 hours of continuous, high-intensity management (3:20 AM Oct 20 to 2:50 PM the following day)
- **Visibility:** Tracking hundreds of impacted assets, managing 200+ recovery tasks, and maintaining DORA-compliant communications
- **Senior stakeholder management:** Directors needed real-time visibility to inform multi-million dollar decisions

---

## The Solution: Cross-Functional Visibility and Orchestration via Cutover Respond

To mitigate complexity, the Bank utilized **Cutover Respond** as the central hub for major incident management. The process unfolded as follows:

1. **Initial alert and handoff:** The incident was logged in ServiceNow, immediately triggering the creation of a dedicated Major Incident Runbook in Cutover Respond.
2. **Centralized control:** An assigned Major Incident Manager (MIM) took the lead, paging out subject matter experts and stakeholders. Cutover Respond became the single platform centralizing all activities.
3. **Structured collaboration:** Defined roles and tiered access allowed representatives from individual business units to join the runbook, adding tasks and users as needed.
4. **Third-party integration:** The Bank invited AWS engineers directly into the runbook, enabling shared situational awareness and coordinated problem solving.
5. **Communications command center:** The response team managed **12 concurrent Zoom bridges** and used integrated third-party chat via the incident runbook, keeping 1,800+ global participants aligned.

## The Outcome: Greater Visibility and Control Under Pressure

**Key results:**

- **200+ coordinated recovery tasks** seamlessly executed
- **Single pane of glass** for all incident activity, eliminating time lost switching between systems
