# Multinational Pharmaceutical Company Uses Cutover to Implement SAP Across EMEA

## Overview

A multinational pharmaceutical company used Cutover to manage the EMEA phases of a global SAP rollout, eliminating 90% of manual communications and successfully completing the most complex wave of the program.

---

## Background: SAP Platform Rollouts Are Too Complex for Spreadsheets

A pharmaceutical company is undergoing a global SAP platform rollout, split by geography due to its size and complexity. After completing 90% of the SAP rollout in the Americas using spreadsheets, disjointed processes, and weekly calls, the Senior Director realized they needed better planning, orchestration, visualization, and communication capabilities for the remainder of the rollout.

---

## The Solution: Cutover Provided the Ability to Rehearse, Manage Communications, and More

The company spent **nine months** planning the EMEA program in Cutover, pulling existing spreadsheet data onto the platform and building runbooks that were 3,000–4,000 lines long, involving over **4,000 tasks and 200 people**.

Key ways Cutover made the difference:

- **Full rehearsals:** Over four to five weeks, the team fully tested the entire plan from start to finish before going live, helping them identify and resolve potential problems ahead of the main event.
- **Executive dashboards:** The C-Suite could easily track program status at a glance, seeing whether it was on schedule and where delays were occurring.
- **90% reduction in manual communications:** Cutover's automated communications removed the need for manual updates during orchestration.
- **Live plan editing:** The team could edit the plan in real time, with everyone instantly seeing changes and adjustments to the critical path — no need to redistribute spreadsheets.
- **Automatic audit trail:** Pharmaceutical companies face significant regulatory scrutiny. Cutover's system of record evidenced every single task without any additional effort.

## The Outcome

Wave one of the EMEA project was a success. The company is gearing up for waves two and three in Cutover, and has also started planning the SAP rollout in Japan using Cutover. They are exploring additional use cases including supply chain manufacturing and chemical plant operations.
