# Global Payments Team in Multinational Bank Improves Ongoing Tech Change with Cutover

**Source:** https://cutover.com/success-stories/multinational-bank

---

## Overview

The Global Payments team in a British multinational bank used Cutover to orchestrate 29,000 changes across its continuous transformation program, reducing failed or delayed changes by 50% and saving an estimated £15 million per annum.

---

## The Problem

The Global Payments team needed a solution to help it orchestrate the **29,000 changes** associated with its continuous transformation. The team was using manual tools and technical automation to plan and execute releases on various payments platforms, but these were no longer sufficient for the volume and complexity of change.

Change was not observable across the enterprise, making it difficult to avoid conflict and ensure appropriate schedule delivery. As part of governance, the team participated in a weekly cycle of three meetings with spreadsheets distributed and consolidated to support each meeting. This was time-consuming and led to data becoming stale very quickly.

---

## How Cutover Helped

Cutover is now used to manage the change process instead of spreadsheets. Benefits include an improved view of the velocity of change and a reduction of risk around change failure. There is no longer a need to constantly update and distribute spreadsheets — all the information needed is accessible and can be worked on collaboratively.

> "Cutover provided a far more effective enterprise release management capability. Cutover will be the only source we use to review and approve the schedule of change — if it's not in Cutover it's not going in." — Head of Run The Bank Corporate Technology

---

## Results

Cutover enabled the team to eliminate legacy internal applications for managing change conflicts as well as manual processes to manage the book of change. By introducing Cutover, they avoided multiple meetings ahead of releases and avoided potential delays or event failures by having better visibility of potential problems earlier.

Of the failed or delayed changes, approximately 45% were due to execution-led problems. By improving the orchestration of these changes, Cutover was able to **reduce the number of failed or delayed changes by half**, saving an estimated **£15 million per annum**.

> "Without Cutover we would have been ignorant of the upcoming log jam of change right up until one week before...we now have a chance to address issues like this six weeks before and remedy in time." — Implementation Lead

---

## Key Results

- 50% reduction in failed or delayed changes
- £15 million per annum in estimated savings
- 29,000 changes orchestrated through the platform
- Legacy applications for change conflict management eliminated
- Issues now visible 6 weeks in advance (previously 1 week)
