# Cutover Enables Bank to Successfully Orchestrate Preparation for IPO

**Source:** https://cutover.com/success-stories/bank-ipo

---

## Overview

A large multinational bank used Cutover to manage the final 48-hour preparation for three high-profile technology company IPOs, coordinating 20 teams and hundreds of activities across banking and technology organizations.

---

## The Problem

A large multinational bank was managing the IPOs for **three well-known technology companies**. The bank needed to manage all the preparations to ensure readiness for trading on the stock exchange in the last **48 hours of the IPO**. This involved an intricate set of hundreds of activities across banking and technology teams. Although not much public attention is paid to the activities leading up to an IPO, the IPO itself is a highly visible event with **enormous reputational risk**.

---

## How Cutover Helped

The bank had previously used Cutover for release and resilience activities and knew it would also be ideal for managing the complex set of steps involved in preparing for an IPO. They used Cutover to manage:

- All the checks needed before the stock was switched on for trading
- The orchestration of all activity related to these checks
- A single view of all progress leading up to the enablement of the stock for trading
- A central place to manage all communications

Reporting and orchestration of activities was automated, removing manual overhead and risk. The Cutover runbooks orchestrated activities across **20 teams** including bankers, technology staff, and stakeholders.

---

## The Results

Cutover enabled not only the bank but all other parties involved in the IPO to collaborate across their multiple organizations in one place, with visibility of all activity leading up to the IPO. This helped ensure the success of the highly publicized IPOs, which would have caused major reputational and financial damage to all parties if unsuccessful. Cutover enabled them to identify potential issues early and course-correct when needed.

---

## Key Results

- 3 high-profile tech company IPOs successfully managed
- 20 teams coordinated across banking and technology
- 48-hour preparation window orchestrated in Cutover
- Automated reporting and activity orchestration
- Early identification and resolution of potential issues
