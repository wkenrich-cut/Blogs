# Major Hotel Chain Merger Successfully Executed on Cutover

**Source:** https://cutover.com/success-stories/hotel-chain-merger

---

## Overview

Two major hotel chains executed a complex merger involving 100 million external users, 300,000 internal users, 17 systems, 458 releases, and 20 million lines of code — all successfully orchestrated with Cutover.

---

## The Problem

Two major hotel chains underwent a merger that involved:

- **100 million external users**
- **300,000 internal users**
- **17 systems**
- **458 releases**
- **20 million lines of code**
- **$140 million spent on software development**

A third-party consulting firm was brought in to shape and support the change in systems implementation as well as a cutover strategy for all impacted IT systems within the reservations and loyalty business units. For such a complex initiative, a powerful, collaborative tool was required to bring together all the data and tasks in one place. The client asked the consulting team for the most innovative and market-leading solution for orchestrating human and machine activities — they chose Cutover.

---

## How Cutover Helped

The hotel chain initially started the merger with many manual processes and adopted Cutover **eight weeks before the first major go live**. Cutover enabled them to properly prepare for and manage the complex set of interdependent technology changes, providing comprehensive event orchestration and greater control. All tasks were managed down to the second and progress could be continuously monitored by stakeholders.

Some of the events in the merger involved up to **500 people** and they enjoyed using Cutover to rehearse events, which helped to continuously improve and refine plans. The visibility into processes and comprehensive audit trail allowed teams to better understand progress during the extremely complex event.

---

## Results

The cutover activities were simulated multiple times, and the actual cutover weekend was a huge success. The biggest benefit was continuous improvement — all tasks, releases, and communications were logged in real time for analysis and improvement and to provide evidence to responsible executives.

---

## Key Results

- 458 releases managed across 17 systems
- Up to 500 people in a single event
- $140M software development program delivered successfully
- Full audit trail of all tasks, releases, and communications
- Continuous improvement via real-time data capture
