## The Problem: Difficulty with Regulatory Reporting for IT Disaster Recovery

A major financial services company was using an internally-built tracking tool that was over ten years old for its recovery processes. Although it worked fairly well internally, regulators opened up a Matter Requiring Attention (MRA) requiring the bank to improve its processes.

The existing system did not enable the bank to standardize plans and there was no visibility of every application having a recovery plan in place. A major concern for regulators was that Recovery Time Actuals (RTAs) could be input manually up to seven days after an incident so there was no way to verify if the recorded timings were accurate.

---

## The Solution: Collaborative Automation for Recovery

After a few events of migrating and standardizing recovery plans, the team ran its biggest recovery test to date using Cutover. In just **16 months**, the bank had reached a point of maturity that can take years.

They ran an **enterprise-wide IT DR simulation** spanning all their lines of business. The event consisted of:

- More than a **thousand recovery plans**
- **Several thousand participants**
- Over **16,000 individual tasks executed**
- More than a **thousand applications** successfully recovered during the 48-hour event

Stakeholders used Cutover's real-time dashboards to view progress throughout the event, which provided live updates and showed the actual timings as they happened.

---

## The Outcome: Drastically Improved IT DR Processes and Reporting

The event was extremely successful and the bank is now mandating the use of Cutover for recovery testing and completely replacing the previous internally-built tool.

Currently, **95% of application recovery plans** have been turned into Cutover runbooks. Cutover's communications, built-in dependency management, and automated audit trail are all making a huge impact on how they manage and report on disaster recoveries.

### Additional Advantages
