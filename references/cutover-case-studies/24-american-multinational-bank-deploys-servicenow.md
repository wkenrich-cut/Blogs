## Overview

An American multinational investment bank used Cutover to successfully deploy ServiceNow on time after multiple failed prior attempts, and subsequently mandated Cutover for all future releases.

---

## The Challenge: Multiple Delays in ServiceNow Deployment

An American multinational investment bank had experienced multiple delays in the deployment of ServiceNow across the organization due to a lack of governance, ownership, and a clear understanding of the dependencies involved. The release team was using Microsoft Project to manage the deployment, but this tool did not provide the functionality to manage the complexity involved.

The transition to ServiceNow from their in-house configuration management database was a critical goal for the bank to simplify processes, reduce risk, and increase the volume of change they could execute safely. Every month of delay was another month paying for a SaaS product without leveraging its benefits.

---

## The Solution: Cutover Enabled Teams to Work Collaboratively

The ServiceNow deployment plan was migrated from Microsoft Project to Cutover. Key stakeholders were able to review the plans in Cutover in depth to verify dependencies, durations, and reporting requirements.

Once the plan went live in Cutover, the team held **daily standups** to review progress and evaluate the impact of deltas against the plan in real time.

Instead of having a single person chasing 20+ teams for updates and then updating a project plan, each team was assigned their respective tasks in Cutover and **automatically notified when their task was ready to start**.

---

## The Outcome: Deployment Success

Cutover enabled the bank to successfully deploy ServiceNow **on time after a number of failed attempts**. Cutover was subsequently **mandated for use across all subsequent releases** and decommissioning of legacy infrastructure.
