# Major Stock Exchange Reduces Data Center Failover Planning Time by 80%

**Source:** https://cutover.com/success-stories/major-stock-exchange

---

## Overview

A major stock exchange (classified as critical national infrastructure) used Cutover to execute its mandatory data center failover test, reducing planning and preparation time by 80% and proving resilience to regulators.

---

## Regulatory Scrutiny and Real-World Risk

A major stock exchange was required to conduct a data center failover test due to regulatory requirements. This test involved thousands of critical activities to coordinate the transition of thousands of services between primary and secondary data centers.

The team had used spreadsheets for similar activities before but had never executed a test of this scale and complexity. Using spreadsheets left them struggling to orchestrate activities amongst siloed teams. Getting this right was critical not only to satisfy the regulator, but also to be prepared for a real-world incident. The exchange is considered critical national infrastructure, so the knock-on effects of a major outage would be enormous.

---

## The Outcome: 80% Reduction in Planning Time

Cutover enabled the exchange to successfully execute its primary data center failover test. Using Cutover **reduced the amount of planning and preparation time by 80% compared to manual tools and processes**. Those running the event could keep track via the main dashboard in the control room while senior stakeholders monitored the event on their own devices.

Cutover also provided the ability to track performance and monitor the failover of each application with detailed real-time analytics — something that could not be done using spreadsheets.

---

## Protected Against Outages

With Cutover, the stock exchange was able to significantly reduce its level of risk and prove to regulators that it was resilient against outages. It can now conduct full data center failovers **every six months** as coordinating in Cutover has made the process easily repeatable.

The exchange has also expanded the scope to involve more applications, people, tasks, and complexity, enabling standardization across the group and its ever-increasing technology estate.

---

## Key Results

- 80% reduction in failover planning and preparation time
- Successful regulatory compliance demonstrated
- Failovers now conducted every 6 months (previously not feasible)
- Real-time analytics for application-level failover monitoring
- Scope expanded to include more applications and complexity
