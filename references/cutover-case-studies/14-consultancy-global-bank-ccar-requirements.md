# Consultancy Firm Helps Major Global Bank Meet CCAR Requirements with Cutover

**Source:** https://cutover.com/success-stories/consultancy-firm-help-major-global-bank-ccar-requirements

---

## Overview

A consultancy firm used Cutover to help a major global bank meet its CCAR (Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review) requirements after failing to do so for three consecutive years, achieving 40% faster month-end closures.

---

## Background: CCAR Reporting Is Vital but Complex

CCAR (Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review) is a United States regulatory framework introduced by the Federal Reserve to assess, regulate, and supervise large banks and financial institutions. CCAR attestation is a complex end-to-end process that requires a chain of human attestations and needs systemic support.

Cutover was used by a consultancy firm to help a major global bank meet its CCAR requirements after having previously failed to meet them for three years running. This meant they were unable to pay dividends or expand their presence in the US.

---

## The Challenges

CCAR reporting complexity was being compounded due to previous mergers and acquisitions. The European bank had recently acquired a smaller bank in the US that had recently made an acquisition of its own. Two layers of post-acquisition dynamics challenged transparency of where data was being sourced and who owned the systems.

The global distribution of the parties involved added to this complexity, with multiple organizations working in multiple countries who had only been recently introduced to each other after the merger. Different management tools that lacked established lines of communication made organizing CCAR reporting even more challenging.

This lack of visibility and communication created delays. It could take 7–12 days after the end of the month to process a file, so downstream recipients would usually not check for the file until about 15 days after month-end.

---

## How Cutover Made a Difference to CCAR Reporting

Cutover enabled the bank to fill in the gaps and create better visibility, communication, and systems of record for the huge amount of human attestations required for CCAR. By automatically creating a record of all file transfers and data handoffs and making the entire process transparent, Cutover made it easier to accurately report to regulators. This visualization also enabled the team to see where delays were occurring and optimize the process further.

Automated notifications removed the delays between handoffs because there was no elapsed time between one task being completed and the next task starting.

---

## The Outcome

As a result, **month-end closures are now happening 40% faster** than before and, with the right tools and processes in place, the bank is able to meet its CCAR requirements.

---

## Key Results

- CCAR requirements met for the first time after 3 consecutive failures
- 40% faster month-end closures
- Full visibility of file transfers and data handoffs
- Automated notifications eliminating delays between handoffs
- Dividend payments and US expansion re-enabled
