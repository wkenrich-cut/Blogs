# A Fortune 500 Retail Operator Scales Migration

**Source:** https://cutover.com/success-stories/fortune-500-retail-operator-scales-migration

---

## Overview

A prominent US liquid energy supplier and retailer partnered with AWS ProServe and Cutover to migrate 144 servers to AWS, accelerating their data center exit and improving IT agility.

---

## The Problem: Legacy Technology Poses Resilience Risks

A prominent US liquid energy supplier and retailer found their business growth constrained by an outdated technology foundation. Their aging infrastructure and legacy systems hindered their agility, preventing them from adapting swiftly to evolving supplier and retailer industry dynamics. The close proximity of their data centers also posed a significant business continuity risk. Critical innovation efforts, including enhancing customer experience, expanding business reach, and introducing new offerings, were hampered. Furthermore, integrating technology from new acquisitions proved to be a slow and expensive process.

---

## The Solution: AWS Rapid Migration Enables Scalable, Resilient Cloud Transition

Following a comprehensive portfolio assessment by AWS ProServe, which identified established migration patterns like rehosting, the company embarked on the AWS ProServe and Cutover Rapid Migration program to transition their data center assets, including 144 servers, to AWS.

Leveraging the Rapid Migration program with Cutover's automated runbook platform provided the company with:

- Standardized pre-built automated runbook templates, aligned with AWS ProServe's best practices and migration patterns, for consistent wave execution
- Organized migration wave runbooks within a clear folder structure for simplified management
- Visualization of upstream and downstream application dependencies during each migration wave using an intuitive nodemap
- Stakeholder accessibility to critical migration details via a centralized runbook homepage
- A multi-runbook dashboard displaying real-time progress and task status across all application migrations

---

## The Outcome: Cutover & AWS Boost Agility, Cut Costs, and Fuel Innovation

Having already migrated two-thirds of their server infrastructure to AWS, the company anticipated completing their data center exit by late 2025. This transition yielded significant benefits including improved adaptability and efficiency in their energy distribution operations, enhanced IT agility with near on-demand scalability, and a clear path towards IT operational cost optimization.

---

## Key Results

- 144 servers being migrated to AWS
- Two-thirds of server infrastructure already migrated
- Data center exit targeted for late 2025
- Improved IT agility with near on-demand scalability
- Enhanced customer experience and revenue generation potential
